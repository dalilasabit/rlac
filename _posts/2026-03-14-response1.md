Computational text analysis allows researchers to examine patterns in large texts through visualizations. Tools such as Voyant Tools and R make it possible to transform textual data into graphs, charts, and other visual forms. However, visualizations cannot always show their meaning and context clearly on their own. Without the context and the theme of analysis both LLMs and human readers may misinterpret the meaning of a visualization.
For Assignment 1, I analyzed a corpus consisting of two novels from the Harry Potter series by J. K. Rowling as well as three fan fiction texts. For this Response, I will use visualizations I generated for Assignment 1 which include antonym frequency graphs and a heatmap of specific word distribution across texts. I then will ask LLM to interpret these visuals without context and observe how the interpretations change after I provide additional prompts.

Part 1: Description of the Visualizations
Using Voyant Tools and R, I generated several visualizations that represent patterns in my corpus. Two visualizations display the frequency of antonyms appearing across the texts. Antonyms such as “win” and “los*” or “love” and “hate” can reflect thematic oppositions that are common in fantasy literature. Another visualization is a heatmap showing the distribution of those antonyms across different texts in the corpus.
